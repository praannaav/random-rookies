Objective

Identify the processes in Schneider Electric’s Supply Chain where paper is utilized and develop solutions to eliminate these paper processes to have a paperless system deployment in end-to-end Supply Chain.

Requirements

  • Innovative solutions for eliminating the paper usage in processes in Supply Chain and improvement of process through leveraging technology.
  • Easily deployed and cost-effective solutions.

Impacts of Paper Usage

  • Environmental Impact:
    • Paper production is a significant contributor to deforestation.
    • Producing one ton of paper requires 17 trees and approximately 20,000 gallons of water.
    • The paper industry is the fifth largest consumer of energy, accounting for 4% of the world's energy use.
  • Operational Inefficiency:
    • Paper-based processes are slower and prone to errors.
    • Manual handling of paper documents can lead to loss or damage.
    • Storage of paper documents requires physical space and is costly.
  • Costs:
    • The cost of paper includes not just purchasing but also printing, storage, and disposal.
    • Research firm Gartner estimates that as much as 3% of a company’s revenue is spent on paper.

Processes Using Paper

In the Plant

  • Work Orders
  • Quality Control Documentation
  • Maintenance Records
  • Kanban

In the Warehouse

  • Printouts of Purchase order and other related documents
  • ASN and Invoices
  • Transfer Orders
  • Waterspider list
  • Order Picking list
  • Labels

Quantifiable Numbers

  • Environmental Savings: Reducing paper usage by 50% could save hundreds of trees and millions of gallons of water annually.
  • Cost Savings:A company can save up to $10,000 per year in paper-related costs by going digital.
  • Efficiency Gains: Digitizing processes can improve operational efficiency by up to 30%.
